# Case: Managing Diversity and Inclusion at Yelp
**Tags:** #case-study #resolved

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** ManagingDiversityAndInclusionAtYelp.pdf

## 1. Core Dilemma

Rachel Williams, Yelp's first Head of Diversity and Inclusion, must develop a strategy to address systemic lack of diversity in the tech sector and within her own firm. She faces the challenge of identifying effective interventions in the recruitment pipeline and organizational culture while ensuring these changes align with the company's financial goals and growth priorities.

## 2. Key Stakeholders & Incentives

- **Rachel Williams** (Head of Diversity and Inclusion): To debias the hiring process, increase representation of underrepresented groups, and foster an inclusive environment where all employees feel safe being their authentic selves.
- **Jeremy Stoppelman** (CEO of Yelp): To position Yelp as an industry leader in diversity while maintaining the company's financial success and rapid growth.
- **Geoff Donaker** (COO of Yelp): To treat diversity as a core management issue and provide top-level executive support for cultural and process changes.
- **Engineering Hiring Managers ('Stoppelgangers')** (Decision-makers for engineering recruitment): To maintain high technical standards and 'culture fit' while managing a massive applicant pool of thousands for very few spots.
- **Jesse Jackson & Rainbow/PUSH** (External Civil Rights Activists): To pressure tech companies for transparency regarding racial demographics and promote equal rights in the Silicon Valley workforce.
- **Tracy Chou** (External Advocate/Engineer): To force transparency regarding the gender gap in technical roles through open-source data collection.

## 3. Financial Context & Constraints

Yelp grew from 250 employees in 2009 to 4,600 in 2017. In 2014, it had 135 million unique monthly visitors and over 2 million active business listings. The company employed nearly 3,000 salespeople by 2017 and faced high competition for engineering talent against firms like Google, Uber, and Airbnb.

## 4. Class Discussion & Takeaways

The class featured a guest session with Rachel Williams, the protagonist of the Yelp case. The discussion focused on the tactical and strategic implementation of DEI within a high-growth tech environment. Rachel emphasized that DEI roles have evolved from simple representation metrics to navigating complex social and political issues. The conversation also touched on the difficulty of removing bias from interview processes, noting that even masked 'Darth Vader' voice technology failed because interviewers still picked up on gendered communication styles (e.g., 'we' vs. 'I').

### Key Takeaways

- DEI strategy must be attached to the company's overall business strategy to have a lasting impact.
- Identify and align with the most influential leaders, General Counsel, and PR heads to navigate internal politics.
- Allyship is a mutual benefit: it supports minority members while providing allies with learning opportunities and better leadership skills.
- Meritocracy is often used as a trope for homogeneity; true meritocracy should naturally result in diversity because talent is distributed globally.
- Avoid 'virtue signaling'; companies should only weigh in on social issues where they have credibility and the ability to move the needle.

### Student Perspectives

- Students questioned the effectiveness of DEI targets vs. quotas and whether they lower standards.
- A student shared an experience in Germany where they felt forced to 'assimilate' rather than being truly 'included.'
- Questions were raised about how to protect DEI programs from being politicized and why progress in tech representation has stagnated since 2005.
- There was a debate on whether companies have a social responsibility to prioritize diversity over immediate financial performance.

### Professor Insights

- The professor noted that many DEI benefits are lost if organizations focus on diversity (hiring) without addressing inclusion (retention).
- He highlighted the 'Business Case for Diversity' vs. the 'Moral Case,' suggesting that younger generations increasingly care about ethical alignment.
- He corrected the notion that DEI lowers standards, suggesting that increasing diversity can maintain or improve quality by expanding the talent pool.
- The professor emphasized that allyship involves active listening and advocacy rather than just passive support.
