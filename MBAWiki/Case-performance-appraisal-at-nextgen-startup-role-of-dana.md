# Case: Performance Appraisal at NextGen Startup: Role of Dana
**Tags:** #case-study #resolved

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** DANA - NextGen Startup Performance Appraisal.pdf

## 1. Core Dilemma

Dana must deliver a constructive performance appraisal to Kendall, a technically brilliant early employee whose abrasive management style, lack of cooperation, and poor team development are causing high turnover and threatening the company's transition to a mature organization.

## 2. Key Stakeholders & Incentives

- **Dana** (Co-founder and Manager): Scaling the company, maintaining morale, and developing a mature management layer that embodies the 'founder's perspective'.
- **Kendall** (Project Manager): Gaining access to 'exciting' and 'interesting' technical work; maintaining high productivity through narrow task assignment.
- **Kendall's Direct Reports** (Team members): Professional development and respectful treatment; currently incentivized to leave due to Kendall's brusque management.
- **Peer Managers** (Other Project Managers): Mutual technical support and collaborative problem-solving across product lines.

## 3. Financial Context & Constraints

No specific financial data provided.

## 4. Class Discussion & Takeaways

The class engaged in a role-play exercise between Dana (the manager) and Kendall (the employee) to simulate a performance appraisal. The discussion focused on how to deliver constructive feedback to a high-performing technical expert who is struggling with team management and peer relationships. The simulation highlighted the tension between an employee's expectation for promotion and a manager's concern regarding soft skills and organizational fit.

### Key Takeaways

- Performance appraisals should ideally contain 'no surprises' if frequent feedback is given throughout the year.
- Separating the evaluation (rating/pay) from the coaching (development) discussion can make employees more receptive to growth-oriented feedback.
- Managers must be specific and future-oriented to turn a difficult conversation into a win-win growth opportunity.

### Student Perspectives

- Some 'Kendalls' felt demotivated when a promotion path wasn't clearly defined, leading to potential turnover.
- Students acting as 'Dana' experimented with starting the meeting with personal check-ins or asking Kendall to self-evaluate first to foster a coaching environment.
- A student mentioned using an external job offer as leverage during the role-play to force a promotion timeline.

### Professor Insights

- The professor emphasized that feedback is a reward and should be treated as an opportunity for growth.
- He highlighted the 'Rater Effect,' noting that half of the variance in performance ratings is often a result of the person doing the rating rather than the person being rated.
- Standardization and 360-degree reviews are tools used to mitigate individual rater bias and increase perceived fairness.
