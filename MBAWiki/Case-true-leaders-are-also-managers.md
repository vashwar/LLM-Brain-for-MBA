# Case: True Leaders Are Also Managers
**Tags:** #case-study #unresolved

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** TrueLeadersAreAlsoManagers.pdf

## 1. Core Dilemma

The artificial separation of 'leadership' (visionary strategy) from 'management' (operational execution) causes executives to become detached from organizational realities. This disconnect leads to unrealistic goal-setting, failed implementations, and the unfair scapegoating of subordinates who are tasked with executing impossible or ill-conceived strategies.

## 2. Key Stakeholders & Incentives

- **Robert I. Sutton** (Author and Stanford Professor): Aims to promote evidence-based management and bridge the 'knowing-doing gap' by integrating operational details with strategic leadership.
- **Warren Bennis** (Leadership Scholar): Popularized the distinction between management ('doing things right') and leadership ('doing the right thing').
- **Project Managers** (Operational Implementers): Responsible for executing executive visions but often face impossible deadlines and resource constraints due to leadership's lack of operational knowledge.
- **Senior Executives (e.g., Unnamed Cell Phone CEO)** (Strategic Visionaries): Motivated by 'big picture' ideas and strategy, often viewing operational details as tasks that are beneath their role.
- **Effective Operational Leaders (e.g., Steve Jobs, Anne Mulcahy, Bill George)** (Exemplars of Integrated Management): Succeed by combining high-level vision with deep immersion in industry details and customer/patient experiences.
