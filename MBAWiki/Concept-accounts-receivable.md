# Accounts Receivable

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 04.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (2).txt

## Definition

A balance sheet account representing money owed to a business by customers for goods or services delivered on credit, reported net of estimated uncollectible amounts.

## Key Points

- Reported net of the Allowance for Uncollectible Accounts (bad debt reserve).
- Bad debt expense is estimated based on historical experience, economic conditions, and credit ratings.
- Actual write-offs reduce the allowance account, not the income statement directly, to avoid double-counting.
- Underestimating bad debt can be used for earnings management to 'borrow' income from future periods.

## Formulas & Equations

- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) = Average Accounts Receivable / Average Daily Sales
- Ending Allowance = Beginning Allowance + Bad Debt Expense - Actual Defaults (Write-offs)

## Examples

- Pfizer reported accounts receivable net of a $449 million allowance.
