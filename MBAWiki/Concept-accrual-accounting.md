# Accrual Accounting

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Article01.pdf, Article04.pdf, Session 02.pdf, Session 01.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States).txt, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting on 1_25_25_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

An accounting method where revenues are recorded when earned (goods/services delivered) and expenses are recorded when incurred, regardless of when cash is actually received or paid. It aims to match expenses with the revenues they help generate.

## Key Points

- Revenues are recognized upon delivery of goods or services.
- Expenses are matched to the period in which the associated revenues are recorded (matching principle) or when resources are utilized.
- Decouples financial reporting from the timing of cash flows, providing a more accurate view of periodic performance.
- Prevents manipulation of financial results through the timing of cash payments.
- Requires adjustments for non-cash items and working capital changes.
- Required by GAAP.

## Formulas & Equations

- Cash from operations = Net Income + [[Depreciation]] - Change in (non-cash) [[Working Capital]]

## Examples

- Allocating the cost of a factory over its useful life via [[Depreciation]].
- Recording a sale on credit as revenue before cash is collected.
- Recognizing wage expenses when employees earn them (even if paid in the next quarter).
- Recognizing revenue upon service delivery even if payment is delayed.
- Recording employee compensation as an expense when work is performed.

## Notes

Focuses on the timing of economic activity. It is the foundation for reconciling net income to cash flows and is essential for providing a true reflection of economic activity.
