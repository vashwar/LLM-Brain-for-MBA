# Adverse Selection

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making 9_28_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A situation where asymmetric information causes the higher-risk or lower-quality participants to be more likely to participate in a market, potentially causing the market to unravel.

## Key Points

- Hidden types or quality differences before the transaction occurs
- High-quality goods (plums) may drop out of the market because buyers cannot distinguish them from low-quality goods (lemons)
- Can lead to market failure where only the lowest quality participants remain

## Examples

- Used car market (Market for Lemons)
- Health insurance pools where only the sickest are likely to sign up at average rates
