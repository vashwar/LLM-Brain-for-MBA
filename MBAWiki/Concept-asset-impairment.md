# Asset impairment

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 05.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025) on 2_22_2025 (Sat)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition
A permanent reduction in the value of an asset (PPE or intangibles) recorded when the asset's fair value (based on future cash flows) falls below its net book value.

## Key Points
- A one-way adjustment (conservative); assets are written down but rarely written up.
- Requires periodic testing (at least annually for goodwill).
- Reduces the asset's book value and is reported as an operating expense.
- Often triggered by restructuring, changes in business environment, or an admission that an acquisition/asset is underperforming original expectations.
- Write-downs result in immediate hits to retained earnings.

## Formulas & Equations
- Impairment Charge = Carrying Value - Fair Value (if Fair Value < Carrying Value)

## Examples
- Dean Food Company's $13.7M write-down of production facilities.
- Warner Bros. Discovery's goodwill write-down.
- Intel inventory write-down.
