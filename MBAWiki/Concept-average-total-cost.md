# Average Total Cost

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 4 Weekend-1.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The total cost divided by the number of units produced, representing the average cost per unit.

## Key Points

- Composed of Average Fixed Costs and Average Variable Costs.
- As quantity increases, average fixed costs diminish (spreading the cost).
- Generally U-shaped due to initial decline in fixed cost and eventual rise in marginal costs.
- Marginal cost intersects average total cost at its minimum point.
- Helps determine unit-level profitability.

## Formulas & Equations

- ATC = (Fixed Costs + Variable Costs) / Q

## Examples

- Unit cost of manufacturing radios.
- iPhone manufacturing costs: high initial R&D divided over millions of units.
