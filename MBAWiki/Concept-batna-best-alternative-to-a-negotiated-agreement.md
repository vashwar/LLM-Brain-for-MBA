# BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_9_WE.pdf, Chapter10MakingRationalDecisionsInNegotiations.pdf

## Definition

The most advantageous alternative course of action a party can take if negotiations fail and an agreement cannot be reached.

## Key Points

- BATNA is the primary source of power and serves as the fundamental leverage in any negotiation.
- You always have a BATNA, even if it is just remaining in your current state.
- A negotiator should always strive to improve their BATNA before starting a negotiation.
- It provides a lower bound for the minimum outcome required from a negotiation and determines the negotiator's reservation point.

## Examples

- If you are negotiating a salary for a new job offer of $90,000, and you have another standing offer for $95,000, the $95,000 offer is your BATNA.
- If a car buyer can purchase the same model from a different dealer for $20,000, that $20,000 is their BATNA for the current negotiation.
