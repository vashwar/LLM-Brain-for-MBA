# Beneish M-score

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Article07.pdf

## Definition

A mathematical model developed by Messod Beneish used to predict the likelihood of earnings manipulation by a company. It utilizes financial statement ratios to detect potentially aggressive accounting practices.

## Key Points

- High aggregate scores in the market often correlate with upcoming economic downturns.
- Acts as a red flag for investors to scrutinize financial disclosures more closely.
- Reflects non-traditional earnings metric behaviors.

## Examples

- Used to identify companies that inflate earnings via accounting shifts rather than organic growth.
