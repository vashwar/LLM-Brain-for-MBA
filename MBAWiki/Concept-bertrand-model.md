# Bertrand Model

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 6 - with notes.pdf

## Definition

An oligopoly model where firms compete by choosing prices rather than quantities.

## Key Points

- If products are identical, firms undercut prices until price equals marginal cost.
- Predicts outcomes similar to perfect competition despite having few firms.
- Firms have no incentive to change prices at equilibrium.

## Formulas & Equations

- P = MC (at equilibrium)

## Examples

- Price war between two identical gas stations
