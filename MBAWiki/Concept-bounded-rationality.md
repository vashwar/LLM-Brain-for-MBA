## Definition

Bounded Rationality is the concept that human decision-making is limited by cognitive constraints, time pressures, and incomplete information, leading individuals to "satisfice" (finding a good enough solution) rather than optimize.

## Key Points

- **Origins:** Proposed by Herbert Simon as a critique of the [[Rational-Economic Model]].
- **Satisficing:** Instead of searching for the absolute best option among all possible choices, humans stop searching once they find the first option that meets their minimum requirements (is "good enough").
- **Cognitive Limits:** Humans lack the mental capacity to identify or process all possible alternatives in a given landscape.
- **Information Gaps:** Decisions are frequently made with incomplete or imperfect data.

## Examples

- Hiring the first candidate who meets the job's minimum requirements rather than continuing to interview all 200 applicants to find the statistical "best."
- Buying a house that is "good enough" because the search process is too exhausting and time-consuming to find the perfect home.
