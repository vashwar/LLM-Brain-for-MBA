# CapEx vs. OpEx

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The distinction between expenditures for long-term investments (CapEx) and short-term operating costs (OpEx).

## Key Points

- CapEx: Costs to buy or build capital assets (e.g., PPE) that provide benefits over multiple periods.
- OpEx: Costs incurred to keep the business running (e.g., rent, utilities) in the current period.
- CapEx is capitalized to the balance sheet and depreciated; OpEx is expensed directly in the P&L.
- US GAAP requires R&D to be treated as OpEx, while IFRS allows some development costs to be capitalized.

## Examples

- CapEx: Building a new factory.
- OpEx: Monthly office rent or R&D salaries.
