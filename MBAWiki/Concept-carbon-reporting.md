## Definition

The disclosure of a company's greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, categorized into Scopes 1, 2, and 3, to inform stakeholders and regulatory bodies about climate risks and environmental impact.

## Key Points

- Scope 1: Direct emissions from company-owned sources.
- Scope 2: Indirect emissions from purchased energy.
- Scope 3: Indirect emissions in the value chain (upstream suppliers and downstream customer product use); these are the most contentious due to measurement difficulty.
- Reporting is currently a mix of voluntary disclosures and emerging regulatory requirements (EU, California), though the SEC proposal is largely stalled.
- Carbon intensity (emissions per dollar of revenue) is used to compare companies of different sizes.
- Greenwashing remains a risk where marketing exaggerates sustainability efforts.

## Formulas & Equations

- Carbon-adjusted earnings = Earnings - (Carbon price x GHG emissions)
- Carbon Intensity = Emissions / Revenue

## Examples

- Apple's commitment to reduce combined Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions by 62% by 2030.
- Tesla's Scope 3 emissions involving lithium extraction.
- Comparing oil companies like Shell/Chevron to tech companies.

## Notes

Data must be reliable and audited to allow for comparison between firms. Companies are currently in a state of confusion regarding conflicting requirements, and this topic is discussed in the context of the SEC's evolving, politically sensitive disclosure rules.
