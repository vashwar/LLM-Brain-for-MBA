# Cash Conversion Cycle

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Sessions 03.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (1).txt

## Definition

A metric that measures the number of days a company takes to convert its investments in inventory and other resources into cash flow from sales.

## Key Points

- Shorter cycles imply better liquidity, more efficient capital management, and less capital tied up.
- Negative cycles indicate the company collects cash from customers before paying suppliers (effectively using supplier credit to fund operations).
- Formula components: DIO (Days Inventory Outstanding), DSO (Days Sales Outstanding), DPO (Days Payable Outstanding).
- Very large companies often use their bargaining power to extend DPO, leading to a negative cycle.

## Formulas & Equations

- Cash Conversion Cycle = DIO + DSO - DPO

## Examples

- Procter & Gamble (negative cycle)
- Apple (negative cycle)
