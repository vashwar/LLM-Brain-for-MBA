# Compliance vs. Commitment

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_10_WE.pdf

## Definition

The distinction between forcing an individual to follow a directive (compliance) versus gaining their voluntary buy-in and long-term engagement (commitment).

## Key Points

- Reward and coercive power typically lead to simple compliance.
- Expert and referent power create more lasting influence and true engagement.
- Persuasion is the primary tool used to move beyond compliance to commitment.

## Examples

- An employee meeting a deadline only to avoid being fired (compliance).
- A team member working overtime because they believe in the leader's vision (commitment).
