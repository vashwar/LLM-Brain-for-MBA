# Confounding Variables

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_2_WE.pdf

## Definition

Extra variables that were not accounted for in a study and can lead to biased results by influencing both the independent and dependent variables.

## Key Points

- They create the risk of 'Self-Selection Bias' if participants choose their own treatment group.
- Confounders can obscure the true relationship between a management intervention and its results.
- Randomization is the primary tool used to mitigate the risk of confounders.

## Examples

- Highly competitive employees might both self-select into a daily quota scheme and naturally have higher sales, making it look like the scheme caused the sales increase.
