# Conjunctive and Disjunctive Events Bias

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** Chapter3CommonBiases.pdf

## Definition

The tendency to overestimate the probability of conjunctive events (events that must all happen together) and underestimate the probability of disjunctive events (events that occur independently).

## Key Points

- Leads to the 'Planning Fallacy' where the time and cost of multi-stage projects are underestimated.
- Overestimation of conjunctive events happens because the overall probability is lower than the probability of any single component.
- Underestimation of disjunctive events leads to unnecessary pessimism regarding complex systems or safety risks.

## Examples

- Underestimating the time needed for a home remodel because every single sub-task must finish on time (conjunctive).
- Assuming a complex system (like a nuclear reactor) is safe because each part has a low failure rate, ignoring that the failure of any single part causes a total system failure (disjunctive).
