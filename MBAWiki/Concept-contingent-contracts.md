# Contingent Contracts

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** Chapter10MakingRationalDecisionsInNegotiations.pdf

## Definition

Agreements where the final terms are dependent on the outcome of future uncertain events, effectively allowing parties to 'bet' on their differing predictions.

## Key Points

- Builds joint value by exploiting differences in expectations.
- Helps manage biases like overconfidence and the endowment effect.
- Diagnoses disingenuous parties (bluffing).
- Establishes performance incentives.

## Examples

- A TV station agrees to pay a production company more for a show if it achieves higher-than-expected ratings.
