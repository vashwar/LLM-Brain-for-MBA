# Contingent Liabilities

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 06.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (3).txt

## Definition

Potential obligations that depend on the occurrence or non-occurrence of one or more uncertain future events.

## Key Points

- Must be recognized as a liability if the contingency is probable and the settlement amount is reasonably estimable.
- Recognized by recording a liability on the balance sheet and a loss on the income statement.
- Legal departments often prefer delaying the recording of these liabilities to avoid admitting guilt and weakening their bargaining position in settlements.
- Provides a way for companies to signal expected costs of legal or environmental issues.
- Companies often provide boilerplate disclosures for lawsuits where the outcome is uncertain.

## Formulas & Equations

- Provisionary Liability = Best estimate within a reasonable range of outcomes
- Accrual = Estimated loss based on historical experience or actuarial analysis

## Examples

- Pending lawsuits
- Environmental remediation costs
- Product warranty adjustments
- Starbucks coffee spill lawsuit
- BP Gulf of Mexico oil spill settlement
