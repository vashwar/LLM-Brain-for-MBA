# Cournot Model

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 6 - with notes.pdf, Slides Week 7 - with notes.pdf

## Definition

An oligopoly model where firms compete by simultaneously choosing quantities to produce, assuming the competitor's output is fixed.

## Key Points

- Firms choose quantity, not price.
- Includes the use of reaction functions to determine best response.
- Equilibrium occurs where each firm's output is the best response to the other's.
- Equilibrium results in output levels between those of monopoly and perfect competition.
- Incentives to coordinate quantities exist.

## Formulas & Equations

- Reaction function: QA = 45 - 0.5QB
- Inverse Demand: P = a - b(QA + QB)

- Airbus vs Boeing market competition
- Airbus vs Boeing output decisions
