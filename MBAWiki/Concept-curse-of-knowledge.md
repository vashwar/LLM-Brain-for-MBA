# Curse of Knowledge

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** Chapter3CommonBiases.pdf

## Definition

A cognitive bias where better-informed people find it extremely difficult to think about problems from the perspective of lesser-informed people.

## Key Points

- Individuals assume that others possess the same background information they do.
- Experts are often worse than non-experts at predicting the beliefs and tastes of the general public.
- Can lead to significant communication failures in organizations.

## Examples

- Product designers creating high-tech devices that consumers find impossible to use.
- Teachers struggling to adjust lessons to the level of students who lack foundational knowledge.
- Assuming a sarcastic email will be understood as such by the receiver without the benefit of the sender's internal tone.
