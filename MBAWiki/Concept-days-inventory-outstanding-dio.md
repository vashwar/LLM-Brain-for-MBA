# Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 05.pdf

## Definition

A financial ratio that measures the average number of days a company takes to convert its inventory into sales.

## Key Points

- A higher DIO can indicate weak demand or poor inventory management.
- Often used by analysts to spot deteriorating operational performance.
- It is inversely related to inventory turnover.

## Formulas & Equations

- DIO = (Average Inventory / Daily COGS)
- Inventory Turnover = 365 / DIO

## Examples

- Target's increasing DIO during periods of weak consumer demand.
