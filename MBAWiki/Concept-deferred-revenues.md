# Deferred Revenues

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Article04.pdf, Session 01.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A liability account reflecting cash received from customers for goods or services that have not yet been delivered.

## Key Points

- Occurs when cash is received before the delivery of goods/services.
- Initial cash receipt increases both Cash Assets and Liabilities (Deferred Revenue).
- It is a liability because the company owes the service or good to the customer.
- Revenue is only recognized once the performance obligation (delivery of service/good) is met.
- Upon delivery, the liability is reduced and Revenue (Earned Capital) is increased.
- Not recognized as revenue until the earnings process is complete.
- Classified as a current liability if the delivery is expected within a year.
- Often a positive indicator that customers are lining up for products.

## Examples

- Receiving payment in the current quarter for a service to be delivered in the next quarter.
- A subscription service collecting payment upfront before the period of service begins.
- Customer deposits for a car delivery months in the future.
- Prepaid annual service subscriptions.
