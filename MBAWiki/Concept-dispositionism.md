## Definition

Dispositionism is a psychological framework and theory suggesting that human behavior is primarily determined by internal factors, such as an individual's personality traits, feelings, beliefs, and emotions.

## Key Points

- **Origin:** Originates from attribution theory, focusing on internal characteristics rather than situational forces.
- **Stability:** Assumes that personality is a stable set of characteristics and behavioral tendencies that remain consistent across various situations and over time.
- **Attribution Theory:** Associated with how people explain the causes of behavior, often defaulting to internal traits when observing others.
- **Contrast:** Often contrasted with situationism in the debate over why people act the way they do.

## Examples

- Explaining a colleague's late arrival as a result of laziness (internal trait) rather than traffic (external factor).
- Attributing a car accident to someone's lack of driving skill or personality rather than poor road conditions.
- The Sorting Hat in Harry Potter serves as a metaphor for determining innate, fixed personality types that dictate future behavior.
