# EBITDA

**Course:** Financial Accounting

## Definition

A non-GAAP financial metric representing earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, often used as a proxy for operational cash flow and to compare operational profitability across different capital structures.

## Key Points

- Strips out financing (interest), tax, and accounting (depreciation/amortization) decisions to highlight core operating performance.
- Facilitates comparisons across different capital structures.
- Commonly used in M&A analysis.
- Critiqued for ignoring actual capital expenditure needs (the 'tooth fairy' argument) and for ignoring depreciation, which represents real costs of asset maintenance.

## Formulas & Equations

- EBITDA = Net Income + Interest + Taxes + Depreciation + Amortization

## Examples

- Operating Income + Depreciation + Amortization
- Comparing two companies with different debt levels.

## Notes

Examples of 'creative' non-GAAP metrics include 'community-adjusted EBITDA'. While useful, it should not be treated as a perfect proxy for cash flow.
