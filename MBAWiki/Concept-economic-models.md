# Economic Models

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 1 Weekend - with notes.pdf

## Definition

Theoretical frameworks used to simplify the real world to navigate, analyze, and make decisions about complex problems through specific assumptions and logical steps.

## Key Points

- Models are simplifications of reality, inherently inaccurate but useful for navigation.
- They rely on assumptions to reach logical conclusions.
- They serve as tools for navigating complexity, much like maps with varying levels of detail.
- Models are often built upon the rational actor assumption.
