# Effective Interest Rate

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 06.pdf

## Definition

The actual return or cost of borrowing that equates the present value of future cash flows to the initial amount borrowed, reflecting the market-demanded rate of return.

## Key Points

- It is the sum of the risk-free rate and the credit risk premium.
- It is used to calculate periodic interest expense on long-term debt.
- The effective rate is distinct from the stated coupon rate on corporate bonds.

## Formulas & Equations

- Effective Interest Rate = Risk-free Rate + Risk Premium
- Ending Loan Value = Beginning Value + Interest Expense - Interest Payment

## Examples

- Calculating yield on a bond sold at a discount
- Determining interest expense for a bank loan

## Notes

The effective interest rate is the 'true' cost of debt to the firm and the 'true' yield to the investor.
