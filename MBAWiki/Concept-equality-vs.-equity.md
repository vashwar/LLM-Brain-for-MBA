# Equality vs. Equity vs. Justice

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_8_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_14_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A framework used to distinguish between providing the same resources to everyone (equality), providing resources based on specific needs to reach an equal outcome (equity), and removing the underlying systemic barriers that cause the disparity (justice).

## Key Points

- **Equality:** Assumes everyone starts from the same place and provides everyone with the same support, regardless of their starting point or specific needs.
- **Equity:** Acknowledges systemic barriers and involves intentional interventions, such as a "longer ladder" or additional boxes for those further from the goal, to ensure everyone reaches the same outcome.
- **Justice:** Focuses on fixing the system entirely—such as straightening a bent tree or removing a fence—so that everyone has equal access without needing extra support or specialized interventions.
- **Temporal Strategy:** Organizations often strive for equity as a necessary short-term intervention to address current disparities, but the long-term objective is moving toward justice where support is no longer the primary constraint.
- Fairness in process does not always result in fairness in outcome.

## Examples

- **The Fence Metaphor:** People of different heights trying to see over a fence; equality gives everyone one box, while equity gives the shortest person enough boxes to see over.
- **The Leaning Apple Tree:** Two children trying to pick apples from a tree that leans significantly to one side; justice involves straightening the tree so both can reach the fruit equally.
- **Orchestra Auditions:** Providing blind auditions where candidates perform behind a screen to combat gender stereotypes and systemic bias in hiring.
