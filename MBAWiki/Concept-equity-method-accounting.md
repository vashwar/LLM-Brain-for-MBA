# Equity Method Accounting

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 07.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (4).txt

## Definition
An accounting method used by companies to account for investments in other entities where they have significant influence, but not full control (typically 20% to 50% ownership).

## Key Points
- Investor is presumed to have significant influence.
- Not marked-to-market; share of profits is added to operating income.
- Investment is treated as an operational stake.
- The investment is recorded at cost initially and adjusted for the investor's share of the investee's profits or losses.
- Dividends received reduce the carrying value of the investment.

## Formulas & Equations
- Investor Income = Investee Net Income * Ownership Percentage
- Carrying Value = Initial Cost + Share of Net Income - Share of Dividends Received

## Examples
- Walgreen's equity method investments
- An equity investment in a startup or strategic partner
