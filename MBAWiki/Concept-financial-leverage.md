# Financial leverage

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 04.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (2).txt

## Definition

The ratio of total assets to shareholders' equity, representing the extent to which a firm uses debt to finance its operations, which magnifies both potential returns and financial risk.

## Key Points

- Leverage increases ROE if the cost of borrowing is lower than the return on investments.
- Higher leverage increases ROE relative to ROA by using creditor money to generate returns for equity holders.
- There is a "dark side": increased debt levels increase bankruptcy risk, lower credit ratings, and increase borrowing rates.
- A ratio of 1 indicates 100% equity financing.

## Formulas & Equations

- Financial Leverage = Average Total Assets / Average Total Equity
- ROE = ROA * (Assets / Equity)

## Examples

- Borrowing $5,000 at 3% to invest in a 10% return opportunity boosts ROE from 10% to 45%.
- An Amazon leverage ratio of ~3.31 indicates that for every $1 of equity, the firm holds $3.31 of assets, implying $2.31 of debt or liabilities.
