# First-Degree Price Discrimination

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 3 - with notes.pdf

## Definition

A pricing strategy where a firm charges each individual customer their maximum willingness to pay for a product. This allows the firm to capture the entire consumer surplus.

## Key Points

- Also known as perfect price discrimination.
- Requires the firm to have complete information about individual customer valuations.
- Eliminates both consumer surplus and deadweight loss.

## Examples
