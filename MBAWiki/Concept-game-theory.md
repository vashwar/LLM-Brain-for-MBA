## Definition

The study of strategic decision-making where the outcome for each participant depends on the actions of others.

## Key Points

- Used extensively in oligopoly models.
- Players' payoffs are interdependent.
- Nash Equilibrium occurs when no player has an incentive to deviate given the strategies of others.
- Dominant strategies are optimal regardless of the opponent's choice.
- Involves finding equilibrium strategies where no player wants to deviate.
- Can be represented using payoff matrices.
- Repeated games can facilitate cooperation that single-round games cannot.
