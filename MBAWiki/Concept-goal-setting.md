# Goal Setting

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_5_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 8_24_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The process of establishing specific and measurable performance targets to align individual efforts with organizational needs. Goal setting is most effective when it involves autonomy and collaborative design.

## Key Points

- **Joint Goal Setting:** Involving employees in the goal-setting process increases autonomy, commitment, and self-directedness compared to top-down mandates.
- **Specific vs. General:** Specific goals are generally more effective for driving performance than vague "do your best" instructions. 
- **SMART Criteria:** Goals should be Specific, Measurable, Attainable (but challenging), Relevant, and Time-bound.
- **Ethical Guardrails and Unrealistic Goals:** While goals should be challenging, setting "barely unattainable" or unrealistic goals can trigger unethical behavior (e.g., the Wells Fargo cross-selling scandal). Joint goal setting helps ensure targets are realistic and attainable.
