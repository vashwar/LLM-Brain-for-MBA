# Goodwill

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 02.pdf, Session 07.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

An intangible asset that arises when one company acquires another for a purchase price greater than the fair market value of the acquired company's net identifiable assets.

## Key Points

- Represents the premium paid for assets like brand reputation, customer lists, or proprietary technology.
- Calculated as Purchase Price minus (Value of Identifiable Assets - Value of Liabilities).
- Only appears on the balance sheet following an acquisition.
- Not amortized, but must be assessed annually for impairment testing to ensure the value still exists.
- Represents premium paid for brand, reputation, or synergies.
- A key signal that a company has pursued inorganic growth via M&A.

## Formulas & Equations

- Goodwill = Purchase Price - (Identifiable Assets - Liabilities)

## Examples

- A company paying $10M for a business whose net identifiable assets are valued at $8M; the $2M difference is goodwill.
- Amazon's acquisition of Whole Foods resulting in a significant goodwill entry ($9.0B).
