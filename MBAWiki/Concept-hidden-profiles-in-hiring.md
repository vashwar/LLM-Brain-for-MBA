# Hidden Profiles in Hiring

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_7_WE.pdf

## Definition

A phenomenon in group decision-making where information is distributed among team members such that no single member can identify the best candidate without aggregating all unique pieces of information.

## Key Points

- Common information has a sampling advantage and is more likely to be discussed than unique information.
- Team members often pay social costs for bringing up unique information that contradicts the group's emerging consensus.
- Groups tend to focus on shared information rather than uncovering the 'hidden' strengths of a candidate.
- Initial public votes can prematurely close off information sharing and lead to suboptimal hiring decisions.
