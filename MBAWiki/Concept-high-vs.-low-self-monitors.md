# High vs. Low Self-Monitors

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** TheAuthenticityParadox (1).pdf

## Definition

A psychological framework by Mark Snyder that categorizes individuals based on their willingness and ability to adapt their behavior to situational demands.

## Key Points

- High self-monitors (Chameleons) are naturally able to adapt to situational demands without feeling inauthentic; they advance rapidly but may be perceived as lacking a moral center.
- Low self-monitors (True-to-selfers) tend to express what they truly think and feel regardless of the situation; they may struggle to evolve their style in new roles.
- Chameleons learn faster by 'faking it' until they find a style that works, whereas True-to-selfers may view such adaptation as 'bogus'.

## Examples

- Investment bankers who borrow styles from senior leaders (Chameleons) vs. those who focus only on technical mastery and view adaptation as manipulation (True-to-selfers).
