# Hindsight Bias

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** Chapter3CommonBiases.pdf

## Definition

The tendency for people to perceive past events as having been more predictable than they actually were before the event occurred.

## Key Points

- Knowledge of an outcome acts as an anchor for interpreting prior judgments.
- Reduces the ability to learn from the past by making people think they already 'knew it'.
- Leads to unfair evaluations of decision-makers based on results rather than their logical process at the time.

## Examples

- Saying 'I knew that was a bad play' only after a quarterback's pass is intercepted.
- A senior VP claiming they could have predicted market research results after they are presented.
- Claiming there was 'plenty of evidence' a supervisor was a bad hire only after they fail to meet production targets.
