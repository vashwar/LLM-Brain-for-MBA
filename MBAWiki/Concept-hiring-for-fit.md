# Hiring for Fit

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_7_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_7_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The practice of selecting candidates based on their perceived alignment with the organization's culture, values, team personality, or stereotypes of success. While intended to ensure cohesion, it is often misused as a proxy for social similarity.

## Key Points

- Hiring for "culture fit" often results in selecting people the interviewers would "like to have a beer with," which leads to increased [[Homophily]] and reduced diversity.
- Over-prioritizing fit can reinforce systemic bias by selecting candidates who simply look and act like the current staff.
- Effective "fit" assessment requires identifying specific, narrowly defined behaviors that actually contribute to organizational success rather than relying on vague feelings of alignment.
- Selecting based on fit frequently results in choosing attractive, extroverted applicants or those skilled in simple behavioral mimicry.

## Examples

- **Social Proxy:** A manager hiring someone who plays the same sport or attended the same university because they feel like a "natural fit" for the team, without identifying behavioral evidence.
- **Mimicry:** An interviewer choosing a candidate because of a shared communication style that mirrors the existing team's internal culture.
