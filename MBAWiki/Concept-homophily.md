# Homophily

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_11_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The tendency for individuals to form social connections with others who are similar to them in terms of characteristics like demographics, beliefs, nationality, or occupation ("birds of a feather flock together").

## Key Points

- **Drivers:** Driven by both opportunity constraints (structural barriers like being in the same neighborhood/company) and personal choice or preference.
- **Information Flow:** Homophily makes people feel comfortable but leads to redundant information and limits access to diverse resources.
- **Systemic Bias:** It can exacerbate systemic issues, such as the gender pay gap, through referral biases and the exclusion of marginalized groups.
- **Social Influence:** Interaction with similar others reinforces existing beliefs, which can lead to echo chambers and increased polarization.

## Examples

- MBA students being statistically more likely to form friendship ties with those in the same section, study group, or citizenship group.
- International conference attendees traveling across the world only to socialize with people from their own country.
