# Ideation Signature (Brokerage)

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** BetterPeopleAnalyticsMeasureWhoTheyKnowNotJustWhoTheyAre.pdf

## Definition

A network pattern where an individual occupies a brokerage position, connecting diverse groups and facilitating the flow of unique information.

## Key Points

- Characterized by 'low constraint,' meaning the individual is not bound by a small, tight network.
- Brokers synthesize information from different teams to create novel solutions.
- Low constraint is a powerful predictor of which employees will generate useful and novel ideas.

## Examples

- A supply chain manager with connections across multiple departments being more likely to propose efficiency improvements.
- A software engineer who talks to multiple 'caves' (isolated groups) in an R&D department.
