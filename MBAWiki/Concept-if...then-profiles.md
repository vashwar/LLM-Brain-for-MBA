# If...Then Profiles

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** PersuadingTheUnpersuadable.pdf

## Definition

A psychological theory suggesting that human personality is a predictable pattern of responding to particular scenarios in specific ways, rather than a set of fixed, unchanging traits.

## Key Points

- Individuals flex their behavior based on the situation (e.g., a dominant manager may become submissive to a superior).
- Understanding these triggers allows for more effective social influence and management.
- Recognizing an individual's specific 'if...then' response helps identify windows of opportunity for persuasion.

## Examples

- A hypercompetitive colleague becoming cooperative when dealing with a high-value client.
- Steve Jobs's narcissistic tendency to reject criticism unless it was preceded by a compliment regarding his influence or intelligence.
