# Income Statement

## Definition

A financial statement that summarizes a company's revenues, expenses, and profits over a specific period of time, reflecting performance using accrual accounting principles.

## Key Points

- Also known as earnings statement, statement of operations, P&L statement, or Profit and Loss statement.
- Uses accrual accounting, which is independent of the timing of cash receipts or outflows.
- Links the [[Balance Sheet]] at the start of a period to the [[Balance Sheet]] at the end.
- Differentiates between core operating performance (COGS, SG&A, R&D) and non-operating items.
- The bottom line represents Net Income (Net Profit/Earnings).
- Includes earnings per share calculation.

## Formulas & Equations

- Gross Profit = Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold
- Operating Income = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
- Net Income = Revenue - Total Expenses
- Net Income = Income Before Taxes - Income Tax Expense +/- Discontinued Operations/Extraordinary items

## Examples

- Applied Materials Consolidated Statements of Operations for fiscal 2017.
- Netflix's revenue recognition for subscription fees.
- Reporting of R&D as a separate functional expense.
- Calculating Gross Profit by subtracting Cost of Goods Sold from Revenues.
- Subtracting SG&A and R&D expenses to arrive at Operating Income.

## Notes

Expenses are recognized via the matching principle to align with revenue generation. Revenue recognition often differs from cash collection timing. This statement focuses on 'flow' rather than a point-in-time snapshot.
