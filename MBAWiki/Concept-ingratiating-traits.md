# Ingratiating Traits

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** CouldYourPersonalityDerailYourCareer.pdf

## Definition

A cluster of dark-side traits characterized by an excessive desire to please others and a reluctance to act independently.

## Key Points

- Includes traits such as Diligent and Dutiful.
- While often viewed positively in subordinates, these traits are problematic for leaders as they inhibit independent action and conflict management.
- Can lead to perfectionism, micromanagement, and submissiveness.

(No formulas)

## Examples

- Dutiful: A manager who is reluctant to express disagreement with superiors or provide negative feedback to reports.
- Diligent: A meticulous employee who becomes so preoccupied with petty details that they obstruct overall progress.
