# Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Rewards

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_5_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 8_24_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A classification of rewards based on whether the motivation is derived from the performance of the task itself (intrinsic) or from external consequences like money or status (extrinsic).

## Key Points

- **Intrinsic Rewards:** Internal satisfaction derived from the task itself (e.g., job interest, sense of meaning, autonomy). These are more sustainable for established employees and keep people working toward a goal over the long term.
- **Extrinsic Rewards:** External incentives such as pay, bonuses, promotions, or recognition. These are highly effective for attracting newcomers or during recruitment phases.
- **Timing:** Immediate rewards are significantly more effective at reinforcing behavior than delayed rewards. 
- **The Reinforcement Gap:** Financial rewards (like year-end bonuses) are often delayed and relatively small compared to the effort exerted throughout the year, making it difficult for employees to link specific behaviors to the outcome.
- **Crowding Out Effect:** Over-relying on extrinsic rewards for tasks that people already enjoy can sometimes decrease overall motivation by eroding the salience of the intrinsic satisfaction.

## Examples

- **Extrinsic/Delayed:** A performance bonus added to a monthly paycheck or a year-end bonus.
- **Intrinsic/Immediate:** The satisfaction of solving a complex puzzle, the sense of purpose in helping a client, or the enjoyment of a challenging project.
