# Learning Goals vs. Performance Goals

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** TheAuthenticityParadox (1).pdf

## Definition

Two distinct motivational orientations: performance goals focus on demonstrating competence and looking good to others, while learning goals focus on developing new attributes and skills.

## Key Points

- Performance goals can inhibit learning because the individual fears looking incompetent during the 'unnatural' phase of growth.
- Learning goals allow leaders to experiment with their identity without feeling like impostors because they don't expect to be perfect initially.
- A 'playful frame of mind' supports learning goals by treating identity as a prototype.
