# LIFO (Last-In, First-Out) Accounting and FIFO Comparison

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Article10.pdf, Session 05.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025) on 2_22_2025 (Sat)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition
Inventory valuation methods that determine the sequence in which inventory costs are assigned to Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). LIFO recognizes the most recently acquired goods first, while FIFO recognizes the oldest inventory items first.

## Key Points
- **LIFO Characteristics:** Used to minimize tax liability by inflating costs during inflationary periods; it does not necessarily reflect the actual physical flow of inventory. In the U.S., it carries a conformity rule: if used for taxes, it must be used for financial reporting.
- **FIFO Characteristics:** Assumes the oldest units are sold first; results in inventory valuations on the balance sheet closer to current market prices. FIFO results in higher income during inflationary periods as older, cheaper costs are assigned to COGS.
- **Inflationary Impact:** During inflation, LIFO reduces reported earnings and taxable income compared to FIFO.
- **Regulatory Note:** LIFO is permitted under U.S. GAAP but is not allowed under International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS).
- **LIFO Reserve:** Represents the difference between FIFO inventory values and LIFO inventory values.

## Formulas & Equations
- COGS_FIFO = COGS_LIFO - ΔLIFO_Reserve
- Ending LIFO Reserve = FIFO Inventory - LIFO Inventory
- FIFO COGS = LIFO COGS - (Ending LIFO Reserve - Beginning LIFO Reserve)

## Examples
- Kroger Co. reporting a $93 million LIFO charge due to rising inflationary costs.
- U-Haul parent Amerco using LIFO to minimize tax payments and maximize cash flow.
- Exxon's inventory valuation using LIFO to manage tax liabilities.
- Auto manufacturers struggling during supply shocks.
- Approximately 50% of S&P 500 companies use FIFO as their primary inventory method.
