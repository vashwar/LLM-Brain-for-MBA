# LIFO Reserve

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Article10.pdf, Session 05.pdf

## Definition
The difference between inventory valued under FIFO (or current cost) and inventory valued under the LIFO method. It allows investors to assess the impact of the LIFO accounting choice on financial performance.

## Key Points
- Required disclosure for firms using LIFO to show how much inventory is understated relative to current costs.
- Disclosed in company notes to financial statements.
- Acts as a reconciliation tool to convert LIFO financials to FIFO for comparability.
- Often analyzed by investors during inflationary periods to understand true business cost structures.
- An increase in LIFO reserve is referred to as a 'LIFO charge'.

## Formulas & Equations
- LIFO Reserve = FIFO Inventory Cost - LIFO Inventory Cost

## Examples
- United Natural Foods reporting a LIFO charge increase from $5 million to $72 million.
- Exxon's 2021 LIFO reserve of $14.0 billion.
