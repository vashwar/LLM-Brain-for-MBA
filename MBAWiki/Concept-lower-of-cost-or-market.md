# Lower-of-Cost-or-Market

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 05.pdf

## Definition

An accounting principle requiring inventory to be valued at the lower of its historical cost or its current market value (net realizable value).

## Key Points

- If market value drops below cost, the asset must be written down.
- The write-down is recorded as an expense, reducing net income.
- Reflects the principle of prudence in financial reporting.

## Formulas & Equations

- Inventory Value = min(Market Value, Cost)

## Examples

- Intel's $723 million inventory impairment for its Optane business.
