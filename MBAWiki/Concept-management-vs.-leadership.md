# Management vs. Leadership

**Course:** Leading People

**Source:** LP_2024_class_1_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 8_10_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A debate regarding whether management and leadership are distinct roles or overlapping competencies. Management typically focuses on the execution of tasks, administrative responsibility, efficiency, and monitoring, while leadership focuses on influencing, strategy, vision, and guiding individuals toward a common goal.

## Key Points

- **The Bennis Argument:** Suggests a sharp distinction where leaders "do the right thing" (vision and direction) while managers "do things right" (efficiency and execution).
- **The Sutton Argument:** Argues that the distinction is often overstated and that effective leaders must also be competent managers because the "devil is in the details" of execution; a vision without the ability to manage the details of its implementation is likely to fail.
- Leading involves influencing, guiding in a direction, or shaping opinions; managing involves bringing about, accomplishing, and having responsibility for conducting operations.
- Effective leadership often requires situational adjustments, moving between high-level vision and the operational details of management depending on the organizational need.
- The best leaders integrate both competencies, ensuring their vision is supported by sound execution.

## Examples

- Robert Sutton suggests that leadership and management are inseparable for organizational success, as leaders must understand the operational realities of their teams.
