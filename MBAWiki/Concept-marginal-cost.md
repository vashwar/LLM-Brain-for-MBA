# Marginal Cost

**Course:** Microeconomics

## Definition

The additional cost incurred from producing one additional unit of output; the change in total cost resulting from a one-unit increase in output or a small change in activity.

## Key Points

- Incremental cost calculation.
- Used to determine the optimal stopping point for business expansion.
- Different from average or total cost.
- Can be calculated as the change in total cost per unit change in quantity.
- Calculus approach uses the derivative of the total cost function.
- Intersects the [[Average Total Cost]] curve at its minimum.
- Rational decision-making involves comparing marginal benefits to marginal costs.
- Optimal net benefit is achieved where Marginal Benefit equals Marginal Cost.
- Fixed costs should be ignored when making marginal decisions.

## Formulas & Equations

- MC = ΔTC / ΔQ
- MC = dTC/dQ

## Examples

- Calculating the cost of the second session of an activity.
- Deciding whether to open an additional coffee shop location.
- Cost of producing one extra iPhone unit.
- Determining the marginal cost of a second gym session when one session costs $26 and two cost $48 (Marginal Cost = $22).
- Expanding a chain of coffee shops until the marginal cost of opening a new location equals the marginal revenue it generates.
