# Marginal Revenue

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 2 with notes.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making (Fall 2024)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The additional revenue generated from selling one additional unit of a product. It is the derivative of the total revenue function with respect to quantity.

## Key Points

- Always less than price for downward-sloping demand; lowering price to sell one more unit reduces revenue on all previous units.
- Equal to marginal cost at the profit-maximizing quantity.
- Can be calculated as the derivative of total revenue with respect to quantity.
- Marginal revenue curves have twice the slope of the inverse demand curve for linear functions.
- Maximizing revenue requires setting MR equal to zero.

## Formulas & Equations

- MR = dTR/dQ
- MR = P * (1 + 1/Ed)
- TR = P * Q

## Examples

- MR curve for linear demand being twice as steep as the inverse demand curve.
- Software downloads where marginal costs are near zero.
- Selling research papers where increasing volume requires significant price cuts.

## Notes

Calculated using a three-step method (Inverse Demand -> Total Revenue -> Derivative). The professor noted that setting MR=0 maximizes revenue, but MR=MC is the target for profit maximization.
