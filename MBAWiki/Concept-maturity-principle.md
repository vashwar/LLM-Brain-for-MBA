# Maturity Principle

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_3_WE.pdf, CouldYourPersonalityDerailYourCareer.pdf

## Definition

The psychological observation that core personality traits tend to shift in predictable directions as individuals age, but generally become stable and difficult to change after a person reaches age 30.

## Key Points

- **Predictable Shifts:** As people age, they generally show an increase in conscientiousness and agreeableness, and a decrease in neuroticism (increase in emotional stability).
- **Stability after 30:** While core traits are resistant to fundamental change after age 30, behavioral patterns can still be modified through self-awareness.
- **Behavioral Modification:** Professional development should focus on taming the expression of [[Dark Side Traits]] in critical situations (reputation management) rather than attempting to rewrite the underlying personality.
- **Active Management:** Meaningful change requires moving from simply "wanting to have changed" to the active, "hard work" of monitoring and adjusting one's behavior in real-time.

## Examples

- A highly "Reserved" individual who, despite their natural personality, commits to speaking up at least once per meeting to manage their reputation as a silent observer.

## Notes

Suggests that while personality is stable in the short run, it evolves over a lifetime. However, because career advancement is a function of how others perceive you (reputation), behavioral change is a necessity for long-term success regardless of core stability.
