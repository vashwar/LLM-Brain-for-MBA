# Monopolistic Competition

**Course:** Microeconomics

## Definition

A market structure with many sellers offering differentiated products where firms have some control over price but no barriers to entry, allowing for some monopoly power while maintaining the possibility of free entry and exit.

## Key Points

- High number of competitors.
- Product differentiation exists (e.g., location, branding).
- In the short run, firms can earn positive economic profits.
- No barriers to entry result in zero economic profits in the long run.
- In the long run, entry of new competitors shifts demand down and makes it more elastic, driving economic profits to zero.
- The long-run equilibrium price equals Average Total Cost.
- Firms maximize profits by setting Marginal Revenue equal to Marginal Cost.

## Formulas & Equations

- MR = MC

## Examples

- Gas stations
- Restaurants in a city
- Retail stores

## Notes

In the short run, it behaves like a monopoly; in the long run, it reaches zero economic profit like perfect competition. It is distinguished from perfect competition by the downward-sloping demand curve and from monopoly by the lack of long-run economic profits.
