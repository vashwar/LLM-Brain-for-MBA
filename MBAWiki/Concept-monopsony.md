# Monopsony

**Course:** Microeconomics

## Definition

A market structure where there is only one buyer or a few dominant buyers, giving the buyer power to influence the market price of a good or service.

## Key Points

- The buyer equivalent of a monopoly
- Buyers can use their power to decrease prices to increase profits
- Concept popularized by Joan Robinson
- Monopsonistic employers can keep wages lower than competitive levels
- Explains why minimum wage increases might not lead to job losses in some sectors
- Market power in hiring allows firms to absorb some wage increases without reducing employment
