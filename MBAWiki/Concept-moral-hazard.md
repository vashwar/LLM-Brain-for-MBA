# Moral Hazard

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 7 - with notes.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making 9_28_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

Occurs when an agent, having private information, takes actions that are detrimental to the principal because the agent does not bear the full cost of those actions. It is the tendency for one party to take riskier actions or decrease effort after entering into a contract, because the potential negative consequences are borne by the other party.

## Key Points

- Arises from hidden information/hidden action
- Often arises after a contract is signed
- Incentives are misaligned
- Includes the failure to maintain assets (like a bike) or exerting less effort when fully insured
- Often managed through incentive alignment, deductibles, or incentive structures

## Examples

- Insurance holders taking more risks
- Employees shirking work
- Mechanics recommending unnecessary repairs
- A cyclist failing to maintain their bike because it is fully insured with no deductible
- Actively managed investment funds that underperform the market while charging high fees
