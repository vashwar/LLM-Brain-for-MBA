# Multiple Attribute Utility Theory (MAUT)

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_4_WE-1.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 8_17_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

MAUT is a systematic, logical approach to riskless choice that involves weighing different dimensions of alternatives and calculating a total utility score to identify the optimal objective answer.

## Key Points

- **Systematic Process:**
    1. Identify relevant alternatives.
    2. Identify dimensions/criteria where alternatives differ.
    3. Weight the importance of each dimension.
    4. Rate each alternative on each dimension.
    5. Calculate totals (Weight × Rate) for each alternative.
- **Timing of Weights:** To avoid [[Confirmation Bias]], weights should be assigned to dimensions before the specific options are known or analyzed.
- **Garbage In, Garbage Out:** The quality and reliability of the final ranking depend entirely on the quality and objectivity of the input data and ratings.
- **Output:** Provides a clear, numerical ranking of alternatives based on calculated utility.

## Formulas & Equations

- Total Utility = Σ (Weight_i × Rating_i)

## Examples

- A professor choosing between faculty job offers by weighting research opportunity (weight 10), location (weight 9), and family needs (weight 8), then scoring each university accordingly.
