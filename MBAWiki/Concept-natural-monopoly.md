# Natural Monopoly

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 5 Weekend notes.pdf, Slides Week 6 - with notes.pdf

## Definition
A market structure characterized by a single supplier offering a product with no close substitutes, protected by significant barriers to entry. A natural monopoly specifically arises because a single firm can supply a good or service to an entire market at a lower cost than two or more firms due to economies of scale.

## Key Points
- Single firm dominates the market.
- Significant barriers to entry exist.
- The firm acts as a [[Price-searcher]] rather than a [[Price-taker]].
- Potential for long-term economic profits due to lack of competition.
- Often result from government exclusive rights.
- Examples include infrastructure-heavy industries.
- Always subject to potential competition from substitutes.
- Often subject to government regulation.
- High fixed costs relative to variable costs.
- Prevents inefficient duplication of services.

## Formulas & Equations

## Examples
- Public utility providers like gas or water services
- Textbook mythical monopoly models
- PG&E (gas pipelines)
- Water distribution systems
- Electrical grids
