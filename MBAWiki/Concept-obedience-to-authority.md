# Obedience to Authority

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_10_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A form of social influence where an individual follows direct orders from an authority figure, often overriding their own moral judgment or personal values.

## Key Points

- **Milgram Experiments:** Demonstrated high levels of obedience where ordinary people were willing to inflict harm (administering shocks) under the direction of an authority figure.
- **Symbolic Authority:** Expert power, often symbolized by artifacts like lab coats, increases compliance.
- **Situational Factors:** Obedience increases when the authority figure is physically present and when the victim is physically distant.
- **The "Greater Good":** The perception that one is working toward a significant study or task influences willingness to comply with questionable orders.
- **Abdication of Responsibility:** Individuals often view themselves as mere instruments of the authority figure rather than agents responsible for their own actions.
- **Lack of Exit Strategy:** The absence of a clear way to leave the situation contributes to continued obedience.
