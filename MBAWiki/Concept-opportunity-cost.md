## Definition

The value of the next best alternative that must be foregone in order to pursue a certain action or investment. It is a critical component in economic decision-making that accountants often ignore.

## Key Points

- Includes implicit costs (e.g., time/earnings foregone).
- Crucial for calculating Economic Profit.
- Distinguishes economic decisions from purely accounting-based choices.
- Focuses on what is given up rather than just accounting costs.
- Economic profit includes the deduction of opportunity costs, whereas accounting profit does not.
- Relevant costs for decision-making are those that differ between alternatives.

## Formulas & Equations

- Economic Profit = Total Revenue - (Explicit Costs + Opportunity Costs)

## Examples

- Earnings foregone during an MBA program.
- Choosing entrepreneurship over a consulting job.
- Forgoing a $150,000 consulting salary to launch a startup with $300,000 in revenue and $200,000 in costs results in a negative economic profit.
- Deciding whether to pursue an MBA requires considering the foregone salary during the years of study.
