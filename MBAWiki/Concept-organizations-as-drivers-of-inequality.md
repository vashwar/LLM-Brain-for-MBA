# Organizations as Drivers of Inequality

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_14_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The theory that organizational decisions—including hiring, compensation, promotion, and algorithmic design—can either reduce or exacerbate societal inequalities.

## Key Points

- Organizations impact lives through 40+ hours of work and the consumption of products/services.
- Inequality manifests in hiring (e.g., representation in orchestras), compensation (stagnant racial pay gaps), and service quality.
- Algorithmic bias occurs when proxies for success or health (like medical spending) encode existing societal disparities.
- Clinical trials often underrepresent women and minorities, leading to higher rates of adverse drug effects for these groups.
