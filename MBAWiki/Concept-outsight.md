# Outsight

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** TheAuthenticityParadox (1).pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 8_17_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

Outsight is the external perspective and self-knowledge gained from taking action and experimenting with new behaviors, rather than relying solely on internal reflection. It suggests that leaders grow by doing, which in turn changes who they are and what they believe they are capable of.

## Key Points

- **Action Over Introspection:** In times of transition, action should precede reflection. New data about the self is only generated through new experiences.
- **Chameleon Concept:** Encourages individuals to be "chameleons"—stretching the limits of their current identity to discover who they want to become.
- **Identity Evolution:** Leadership identity is not fixed; it should change and expand as one moves into larger, more complex organizational roles.
- **Building Outsight:** Achieved by taking on new projects, interacting with diverse networks, and trying unfamiliar ways of getting things done.
