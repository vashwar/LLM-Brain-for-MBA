## Definition

Overconfidence is a cognitive bias where a person's subjective confidence in the accuracy of their judgments, abilities, or performance is reliably greater than the objective accuracy of those judgments. It is often manifested as the "better-than-average effect."

## Key Points

- **Better-Than-Average Effect (BTAE):** Most people in a peer group rank themselves above the 50th percentile on positive traits. This is larger for personality traits (e.g., honesty, mental stability) than for technical abilities.
- **Task Type:** Overconfidence is most pronounced for personality traits and simple, familiar tasks where individuals feel they have high competence.
- **Difficulty Effect:** Confidence is often highest when individuals are answering moderately to extremely difficult questions, regardless of actual accuracy.
- **Mitigation:** Can be reduced through objective benchmarking, perspective-taking, and active self-awareness practices.
- **Link to Confirmation:** Emerges from a failure to seek disconfirming evidence about one's own abilities.

## Examples

- **Driving Ability:** 64% of people rank themselves in the top percentiles for driving ability.
- **Mental Stability:** 68% of individuals rank themselves as above average.
- **Hiring:** Managers believing their "intuition" is infallible despite poor statistical outcomes.
- **Entrepreneurship:** Entrepreneurs remaining certain of success despite very high base rates for business failure.
