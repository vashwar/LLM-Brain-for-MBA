# Perfect Competition

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 5 Weekend notes.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making_Captions_English (United States) (1).txt

## Definition

A theoretical market structure characterized by many small firms acting as price-takers, producing homogeneous products with no barriers to entry or exit. In the long run, firms earn zero economic profits as supply adjusts until price equals the minimum average total cost.

## Key Points

- Large number of competitors
- No product differentiation
- Price-takers
- Individual firms face a flat demand curve
- Profit maximization occurs where marginal cost equals price
- Short-run production continues if price exceeds average variable cost
- Free entry and exit drive long-run economic profits to zero

## Formulas & Equations

- MR = P
- MC = P (for profit maximization)
- P = ATC (for long-run equilibrium)

## Examples

- Corn production
- Beef and poultry commodity markets
- Soybean farming
- 3D printing services
