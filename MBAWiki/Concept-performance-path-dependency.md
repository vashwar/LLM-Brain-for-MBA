# Performance Path Dependency

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** SelectionBiasAndThePerilsOfBenchmarking.pdf

## Definition

A situation where a company's current success is a result of past achievements or luck rather than current management quality, creating a 'head start' effect.

## Key Points

- Past accomplishments can unfairly magnify the perception of current management ability.
- Success can feed on itself through mechanisms like customer inertia and high switching costs.
- Stock market returns can mitigate this bias as they reflect changes in future expectations, though they have their own limitations.

## Examples

- A software firm with high market share in 2004 will likely have high market share in 2005 due to switching costs, even if managed poorly.
- A runner who wins a race and gets a head start in the next race makes subsequent wins less impressive as indicators of raw ability.
