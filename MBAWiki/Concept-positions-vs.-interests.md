# Positions vs. Interests

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_9_WE.pdf, Chapter10MakingRationalDecisionsInNegotiations.pdf

## Definition

The distinction between public claims or specific requests made by a negotiator (positions/the "What") and the underlying needs, motives, or motivations that drive those demands (interests/the "Why").

## Key Points

- Focusing on interests rather than positions allows for more creative, integrative solutions that rigid positions might block.
- Asking "Why?" is the most important tactic for uncovering the other side's interests.
- Information gathering and sharing is a major source of power.
- Trading off issues based on interests is the fundamental key to value creation in [[Negotiation]].

## Examples

- **General:** Position: "I need a $10,000 raise." Interest: "I need to feel valued for my extra workload and ensure my commuting costs are covered."
- **Historical:** In the 1978 Camp David Accords, Egypt's position was "return the Sinai," while its interest was sovereignty. Israel's position was "keep the Sinai," while its interest was security. By focusing on interests, they reached an agreement involving a demilitarized zone.
