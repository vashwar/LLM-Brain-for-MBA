# Positive vs. Negative Feedback

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_5_WE.pdf

## Definition

The strategic use of either reinforcing or corrective information based on the recipient's level of commitment and expertise.

## Key Points

- Positive Feedback: Best for building personal commitment; works most effectively for novices or those not yet fully committed to a task.
- Negative Feedback: Encourages critical reflection; works best for experts or individuals who are already highly committed to achieving a goal.

## Formulas & Equations

(No formulas)

## Examples

- A manager uses positive encouragement for a new hire learning a software, but provides rigorous critical critique to a senior developer who is highly dedicated to code quality.
