# Power in Organizations

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_10_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The ability to influence behavior, change the course of events, overcome resistance, and get people to do things they would not otherwise do.

## Key Points

- **Positional Power:** Formal authority granted by an individual's rank in a hierarchy or the specific position a person holds (e.g., CEO or manager).
- **Reward Power:** The control over desirable intrinsic or extrinsic resources (e.g., bonuses, promotions, or symbolic rewards like medals or branded socks) in exchange for compliance.
- **Coercive Power:** The ability to punish or force compliance through the threat of withholding valued resources or termination.
- **Expert Power:** Influence derived from possessing specialized knowledge, skills, or expertise that is trusted by others.
- **Referent Power:** Influence based on the respect, admiration, loyalty, status, or reputation of an individual (charisma).

## Examples

- A manager using positional power to assign tasks or mandate safety protocols, such as wearing seatbelts on forklifts.
- Using expert power to convince a manager of a technical decision based on deep engineering knowledge.
- A finance professor using expert power to convince students of an investment strategy.

- Jeff Pfeffer's definition of power is central to this concept.
