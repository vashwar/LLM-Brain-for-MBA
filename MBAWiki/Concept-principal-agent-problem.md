# Principal-Agent Problem

## Definition

A situation where a principal hires an agent to perform a task, but their incentives are not perfectly aligned and the principal cannot perfectly observe the agent's actions or information. This conflict arises because the agent's interests differ from the principal's, and the principal cannot perfectly monitor the agent.

## Key Points

- Requires asymmetric information
- Involves misaligned incentives between the two parties
- Often leads to suboptimal outcomes for the principal
- Includes hidden actions (hidden knowledge of quality, effort, or intent)
- Highlights the difficulty of contracting on effort directly, leading to reliance on incentive pay or subjective performance evaluations
