# Profit Maximization

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 2 with notes.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making (Fall 2024)_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The process by which a firm determines the price and output level that returns the greatest profit. The goal is to set the quantity where marginal revenue equals marginal cost to achieve the highest possible profit.

## Key Points

- Necessary condition: MR = MC.
- Producing where MR > MC increases profit by producing more; producing where MR < MC increases profit by producing less.
- Occurs where the vertical distance between Total Revenue and Total Cost is greatest.
- Fixed costs are excluded from the marginal decision-making process.

## Formulas & Equations

- Profit = TR - TC
- MR = MC
- dProfit/dQ = MR - MC = 0

## Examples

- Setting Q=10 when MR=MC=4.
- A pharmaceutical company deciding the optimal price for a new pill.
- BMW choosing price points for cars in US vs. German markets.

## Notes

Requires knowledge of the cost function and demand function. Firms maximize profits by producing where marginal revenue covers the marginal cost; they avoid the 'inelastic region' of demand.
