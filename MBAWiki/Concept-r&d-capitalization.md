# R&D Capitalization

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (3).txt

## Definition

The practice of treating a portion of research and development costs as an asset on the balance sheet rather than an expense in the income statement.

## Key Points

- Capitalizing R&D can inflate earnings and create lower Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratios, potentially making a company look like a bargain.
- Under US GAAP, R&D must generally be expensed (OpEx), while IFRS allows capitalization for the 'Development' phase once technical feasibility and commercial viability are established.
- Comparing companies across different jurisdictions requires adjusting financial statements to a uniform standard (either full expensing or full capitalization).

## Formulas & Equations

- Capitalized Asset = Portion of Development costs meeting feasibility criteria
- Adjusted Earnings = Reported Earnings - Amortization of R&D Asset + Current R&D Expense

## Examples

- Tata Steel capitalizing 80% of R&D versus GM expensing 100% due to US GAAP requirements.
