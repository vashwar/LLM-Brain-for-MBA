# Rational-Economic Model

**Course:** Leading People, Financial Accounting
**Source:** LP_2024_class_4_WE-1.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 8_17_24_Captions_English (United States).txt, Article03.pdf

## Definition

A normative and prescriptive decision-making framework that assumes individuals or entities have perfect information and act as rational agents to optimize their outcomes, interests, and goals by systematically considering all possible alternatives.

## Key Points

- **Assumptions:** Assumes decision-makers are perfectly rational "[[Homo Economicus]]" agents who act to maximize utility.
- **Process:** Requires identifying all possible criteria, weighting them logically, and evaluating every alternative against those weights.
- **Goal:** Aims for the optimal choice rather than a merely sufficient one.
- **Tools:** The model is supported by decision-analysis tools such as [[Multiple Attribute Utility Theory (MAUT)]] and Decision Trees.
- Companies assume that analysts use AI tools to digest earnings data rather than reading full documents.
- Management attempts to align their messaging with how these investor AI tools process information to ensure the 'output' is consistent with corporate objectives.
- This reflects an effort to manage the information environment where investors act as rational agents using automated tools.

## Examples

- Selecting a university by creating a comprehensive list of all options and rating them on a set of pre-determined, weighted dimensions.
- A firm using a cold, data-driven matrix to decide on a multi-billion dollar acquisition based on projected NPV and risk scores.
- Companies proactively testing if their message will be 'spit back' correctly by investor AI analysis tools.

## Notes

- While this model provides a useful benchmark for ideal decision-making, it is often criticized because real humans are frequently irrational and limited by cognitive constraints.
- Investors are increasingly using AI to process information, forcing firms to adapt their communication strategy accordingly.
