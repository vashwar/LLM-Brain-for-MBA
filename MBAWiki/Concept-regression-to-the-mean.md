# Regression to the Mean

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** Chapter3CommonBiases.pdf

## Definition

The statistical phenomenon that if a variable is extreme on its first measurement, it will tend to be closer to the average on its second measurement.

## Key Points

- People often invent causal explanations for what is actually a random statistical fluctuation.
- Ignoring regression leads to overestimating the effectiveness of punishment and underestimating the power of reward.
- Extreme performance usually involves a component of luck that is unlikely to be repeated exactly.

## Examples

- The 'Sophomore Jinx' in sports where a great rookie has a less impressive second year.
- Flight instructors believing harsh criticism improved landings, when the improvement was actually just statistical regression from an unusually bad landing.
- Exceptional corporate profits one year tending to decline toward the industry average the following year.
