# Reporting and Analyzing Liabilities

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 06.pdf

## Definition

An obligation arising from past events that requires a future transfer of assets or provision of services. Long-term liabilities are recorded at the present value of future payments, while current liabilities are generally not adjusted for the time value of money.

## Key Points

- Liabilities represent an obligation to make future payments for benefits received.
- Long-term liabilities utilize present value adjustments to reflect economic substance.
- Contingent liabilities must be accrued if payment is probable and the amount can be reasonably estimated.

## Formulas & Equations

- Present Value (PV) = 1 / (1 + r)^t

## Examples

- Product warranties
- Pending lawsuit settlements
- Bank loans and bonds
