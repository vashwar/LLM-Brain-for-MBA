# Reservation Price

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_9_WE.pdf, Chapter10MakingRationalDecisionsInNegotiations.pdf

## Definition

The "walkaway point," indifference point, or the limit beyond which a negotiator prefers an impasse to an agreement. It is the maximum price a buyer is willing to pay or the minimum price a seller is willing to accept.

## Key Points

- Also known as the Reservation Point (RP).
- It is the point at which the negotiator is indifferent between an agreement and an impasse.
- Directly derived from the value of a negotiator's [[BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)]] plus or minus opportunity costs, switching costs, and relationship factors.
- Determines the boundaries of the bargaining zone or [[Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA)]].
- Should not change unless the BATNA itself or the terms of the specific deal change.

## Formulas & Equations

- Reservation Price = BATNA +/- (Transaction Costs + Opportunity Costs + Subjective Value)
- Reservation Point = Value of BATNA

## Examples

- A homebuyer's reservation price is $550,000 because that is the maximum value they attribute to the house compared to other options.
- An MBA student decides they will not accept any job offer below $93,000; this figure is their reservation price.

## Notes

Reservation prices vary between individuals even if they have the same BATNA because of subjective factors like ego or relationships. Failure to assess this accurately leads to emotional decision-making.
