# Return on Assets (ROA) and Return on Equity (ROE)

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 04.pdf, Sessions 03.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (2).txt

## Definition

Return on Assets (ROA) is a performance metric that measures how effectively a company generates profit from the capital under its control, regardless of how it is financed. Return on Equity (ROE) is a measure of profitability per dollar of shareholders' equity, representing the return generated on the capital invested by shareholders.

## Key Points

- Higher ROA signifies better efficiency in using assets to generate profit; it requires earning high profits while minimizing invested capital.
- ROA reflects company performance independent of financing structure and is used for high-level organizational performance evaluation.
- ROA allows comparison of profitability across companies of different sizes by normalizing for asset base.
- ROE reflects both operational performance and the impact of financial leverage.
- ROE increases with higher debt for a given level of assets (leverage effect).
- Higher leverage introduces increased risk and potential for lower credit ratings.
- ROE is higher when there is more debt and less equity for a given level of assets.
- Evaluates how effectively management uses investors' capital.

## Formulas & Equations

- ROA = Net Income / Average Total Assets
- ROE = Net Income / Average Total Equity

## Examples

- Amazon's ROA calculated as 9.0% using net earnings divided by average assets.
- Amazon's ROE calculated as 28.8% based on average equity.
- Company A with $10M assets and $1M net income has an ROA of 10%.

## Notes

ROA measures operational efficiency, while ROE incorporates the effects of capital structure (debt vs. equity financing) on returns to shareholders. ROA is often used in conjunction with ROE to evaluate capital usage. Companies also use these metrics to evaluate the success of acquisitions.
