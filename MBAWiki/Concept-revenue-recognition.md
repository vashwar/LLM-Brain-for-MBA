# Revenue Recognition

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 04.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (2).txt

## Definition

The accounting principle that determines when a company can record revenue on its income statement, typically when goods or services are delivered to the customer.

## Key Points

- Bundled sales must be separated into distinct components that can be valued on a stand-alone basis; revenue for each component is recognized as it is delivered.
- FASB provides guidelines that evolve with changing business models.
- Must account for sales returns by taking a 'haircut' on recognized revenue based on historical data.
- Upfront payments for services (like gift cards or memberships) must be deferred and recognized over the service period.
- Companies must disclose their specific recognition policies due to differing business models (e.g., retail vs. SaaS).
