# Reverse Causality

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** SelectionBiasAndThePerilsOfBenchmarking.pdf

## Definition

A logical error in management analysis where a practice is identified as the cause of success, when in reality, success provided the resources to implement the practice.

## Key Points

- High performance allows firms to afford expensive or time-consuming programs that struggling firms cannot.
- It is easier to build team-based cultures or implement quality programs in a healthy, wealthy firm than in a failing one.
- Managers may associate early market entry with success because only strong firms with high capabilities choose to enter early.

## Examples

- The association between strong culture and high performance: Success may lead to a strong culture rather than vice versa.
- First-mover advantage being credited for success when it was actually the firm's superior distribution capabilities that allowed it to be a first mover successfully.
