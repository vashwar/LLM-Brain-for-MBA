# Risky Business Practices and Performance Volatility

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** SelectionBiasAndThePerilsOfBenchmarking.pdf

## Definition

Practices that increase the variance of outcomes, leading to either extreme breakthroughs or extreme failures, which are often misinterpreted as universally positive due to selection bias.

## Key Points

- Risky practices may have a negative average effect on performance while producing spectacular individual successes.
- Selection bias causes managers to see only the 'spikes' in performance and miss the 'plummets' of firms that went out of business.
- The observed correlation between a risky practice and performance can appear positive in a survivor-only sample, even if the true correlation is negative.

## Examples

- Cross-functional teams: While they produce breakthroughs, Fleming's study found they also produce more failures than homogeneous teams on average.
- Gut-based decision making: Celebated leaders like Jack Welch or Akio Morita are 'survivors' of risky, intuition-led strategies that led many others to failure.
