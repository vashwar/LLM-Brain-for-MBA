## Definition

A form of price discrimination where a market is divided into distinct groups based on observable characteristics, and different prices are charged to each group. It is a pricing strategy where a firm charges different prices for the same product to different groups of customers based on their price elasticity of demand.

## Key Points

- Also known as third-degree price discrimination.
- Firms charge lower prices to more price-elastic segments.
- Requires a mechanism to verify segment membership to prevent arbitrage.
- Useful when marginal costs are similar but elasticities differ.
- Allows firms to capture more consumer surplus.
- Helps avoid wasting social resources on low-value consumption.
