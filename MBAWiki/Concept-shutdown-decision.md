# Shutdown Decision

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 5 Weekend notes.pdf, Slides Week 6 - with notes.pdf

## Definition
The short-run decision a firm makes when it is losing money, determining whether to continue production or cease operations entirely.

## Key Points
- Fixed costs are ignored if they are sunk in the short run and do not impact the decision.
- Compare Price to Average Variable Cost (AVC).
- Continue operating if Price > Average Variable Cost.
- Shut down if Price < Average Variable Cost.

## Formulas & Equations
- P > AVC = Continue
- P < AVC = Shut down

## Examples
- Movie production continuing to recover sunk costs
- A firm losing money on total cost but covering variable costs
