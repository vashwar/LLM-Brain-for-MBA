# Simple vs. Complex Contagions

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_11_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

Different mechanisms for the spread of information or behaviors across a network based on the level of social reinforcement required for adoption.

## Key Points

- **Simple Contagion:** Information or behaviors that require only one contact for transmission (e.g., a viral joke, a news item, or simple information about a job opening).
- **Complex Contagion:** Behaviors, cultural norms, or high-risk changes that require reinforcement from multiple sources to be adopted (e.g., committing to a major organizational change initiative or adopting a new habit).
- **Network Strategy:** To spread simple contagions, one should target highly connected nodes (hubs). To spread complex contagions, one should focus on "wide bridges" or clusters with strong ties where reinforcement is high.

## Examples

- **Simple:** Hearing about a local event or a basic news headline.
- **Complex:** Adopting a new CRM system at work, which usually requires hearing about its value from multiple respected peers before one is willing to change their workflow.
