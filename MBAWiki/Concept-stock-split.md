# Stock Split

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 07.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (4).txt

## Definition
A corporate action in which a company divides its existing shares into multiple shares to lower the trading price per share without changing the market capitalization.

## Key Points
- Has no accounting effect on dollar amounts in equity accounts.
- Increases the count of outstanding shares proportionally.
- Used to improve market liquidity.
- The size of the 'pie' remains the same; it is simply divided into more slices.
- Economic effect is null; ownership percentage remains unchanged.
- Often performed when the stock price reaches high levels to increase liquidity and accessibility for small investors.

## Formulas & Equations
- Post-split Price = Pre-split Price / Split Ratio (or Split Factor)

## Examples
- Nvidia's 10-for-one stock split in 2024
- Apple and Tesla stock splits
- A 10-for-1 split where a $1,100 share becomes ten $110 shares

## Notes
- Does not change the fundamental value of the company.
- Companies use these to attract small investors despite fractional share trading options.
