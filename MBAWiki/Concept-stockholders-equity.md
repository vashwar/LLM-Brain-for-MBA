# Stockholders' Equity

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 07.pdf

## Definition

The section of the balance sheet that represents the book value of shareholders' investment in the company. It consists of contributed capital from investors and earned capital retained by the company.

## Key Points

- Divided into contributed capital and earned capital (retained earnings).
- Market value of equity is determined by supply and demand, distinct from book value.
- Includes contra-equity accounts like treasury stock.

## Formulas & Equations

- Stockholders' Equity = Contributed Capital + Retained Earnings + Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income - Treasury Stock

## Examples

- Nvidia's common stock reporting
- Alphabet's multiple classes of stock
