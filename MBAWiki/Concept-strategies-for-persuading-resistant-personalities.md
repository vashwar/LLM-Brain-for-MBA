# Strategies for Persuading Resistant Personalities

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_10_WE.pdf, Chapter10MakingRationalDecisionsInNegotiations.pdf, PersuadingTheUnpersuadable.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A framework for tailored communication and tactical approaches designed to influence individuals who display rigid personality traits—such as overconfidence, stubbornness, narcissism, or disagreeableness—by identifying and leveraging their situational behavioral patterns to unlock value.

## Key Points

- **Managing Resistant Personalities (Adam Grant Framework):**
    - **The Overconfident (Know-it-all):** Ask them to explain the mechanics of how their idea works. This reveals [[The Illusion of Explanatory Depth]], exposing their knowledge gaps and making them more receptive to information.
    - **The Stubborn:** Avoid direct pressure. Treat the proposal as a "game of catch" where you seed ideas and let them shape the conclusion to maintain their sense of control and ownership.
    - **The Narcissist:** Provide praise in a domain unrelated to the change you are seeking. This bolsters their ego and reduces defensiveness during the actual critique or pivot.
    - **The Disagreeable:** Be prepared to argue and stand your ground. They often respect "scrappy" peers who push back, viewing conflict as a tool to refine and test the strength of an idea.
- **Tactics for Unlocking Value:**
    - **Build Trust and Share Information:** Reduces the fear of exploitation.
    - **Ask Questions:** Gathers information on interests rather than just positions.
    - **Strategically Disclose Information:** Incremental sharing to trigger reciprocity.
    - **Negotiate Multiple Issues Simultaneously:** Prevents early lock-in on single issues.
    - **Make Multiple Offers Simultaneously (MESOs):** Presenting offers of equal value to yourself to learn the other side's priorities.

## Examples

- **Revealing Gaps:** Asking an overconfident PhD job candidate to explain a complex concept on a whiteboard to reveal knowledge gaps. Similarly, Wendell Weeks (Corning) teaching Steve Jobs the science of glass.
- **Ownership:** Mike Bell planting seeds for Apple TV by asking "What if?" questions rather than demanding a box be built.
- **Lowering Stakes:** Framing a new initiative as a "pilot" to make a stubborn leader more willing to try it.
- **Persistence:** Tony Fadell using prototypes and persistence to overcome Steve Jobs's resistance to the iPhone.

## Notes

- These strategies address common psychological barriers and help overcome the "Fixed-Pie" bias in System 1 thinking.
- Adam Grant's article "Persuading the Unpersuadable" highlights that these tactics should often be used privately with leaders to avoid triggering public defensiveness.
