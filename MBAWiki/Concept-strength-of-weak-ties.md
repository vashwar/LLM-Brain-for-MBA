# Strength of Weak Ties

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_11_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

The theory that acquaintances (weak ties) are more valuable than close friends (strong ties) for accessing novel information, job opportunities, and bridging different social circles.

## Key Points

- **Novelty:** Weak ties serve as bridges to different social circles, whereas strong ties (close friends/family) often share the same redundant information.
- **Job Search:** Mark Granovetter's seminal 1970s study found that most people find jobs through weak ties rather than strong ones.
- **LinkedIn Research:** A 2022 large-scale LinkedIn experiment confirmed that "moderately" weak ties are the most effective for job mobility.
- **Benefits:** Weak ties are associated with higher creativity, innovation, faster promotions, and wage increases.

## Examples

- Receiving a significant job lead from a former colleague you haven't spoken to in two years rather than from your best friend.
- **Paul Revere:** His diverse network of acquaintances (weak ties) allowed him to mobilize a militia more effectively than William Dawes, whose network was less diverse.
