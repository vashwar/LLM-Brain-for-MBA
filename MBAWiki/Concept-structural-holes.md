# Structural Holes

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_11_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_21_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

Gaps between clusters of individuals or groups in a social network that provide opportunities for a third party to act as a bridge or broker.

## Key Points

- **Brokerage:** Individuals who bridge structural holes act as brokers between disconnected groups. 
- **Information Access:** Brokers tend to have access to more diverse, novel, and non-redundant information.
- **Creativity and Innovation:** Brokerage allows for the synthesis of ideas from different parts of the organization, leading to higher creativity.
- **Performance Benefits:** Ron Burt's research correlates brokerage with faster promotions, higher performance evaluations, and larger raises.
- **Constraint:** The opposite of brokerage. High constraint means an individual's contacts are already connected to each other, resulting in redundant information and limited influence.

## Examples

- **Paul Revere:** His network featured high diversity and low redundancy, allowing him to act as a broker between different town leaders during the American Revolution.
- **Project Management:** A manager sitting between a customer group and an engineering team.
- **Ideation Signature:** An individual network pattern with many non-redundant contacts.

## Notes

- While powerful, brokerage positions represent a "vulnerable signature" for an organization; if the broker leaves, the network becomes disconnected.
- A network with high constraint usually has fewer structural holes.
