# Structured vs. Unstructured Interviews

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_7_WE.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 205-LEC-31A _ 32A - Leading People 9_7_24_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

A comparison between two interviewing methods: unstructured interviews are conversational, non-standardized, and inconsistent, whereas structured interviews use standardized, work-relevant questions and a systematic rating scale for every candidate.

## Key Points

- Unstructured interviews are weak predictors of job performance and are highly susceptible to perceptual biases, such as the similarity error and the halo effect.
- Structured interviews ensure consistency and fairness by asking the same behavioral questions and using work-relevant criteria for all candidates.
- Standardized rating systems and multiple interviewers who calibrate their ratings improve the validity of the selection process.
- Structured interviews reduce the likelihood of asking illegal or biased questions regarding race, religion, or marital status.
- Statistically, structured interviews are among the highest predictors of job performance, whereas years of experience are surprisingly low predictors.

## Examples

- **VA website sample questions:** Using behavioral-based prompts derived from established sources rather than casual, hypothetical 'get to know you' conversations.
- **Systematic Rating:** Scoring responses based on a defined rubric rather than relying on a general "feeling" or intuition about a candidate.

## Notes

- While unstructured interviews remain popular because people believe they are "good judges of character," they are objectively inferior in predictive power.
