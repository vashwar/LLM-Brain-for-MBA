# Sunk Costs

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 4 Weekend-1.pdf, Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making_Captions_English (United States).txt

## Definition

Costs that have already been incurred and cannot be recovered regardless of future decisions. These should be ignored in rational future decision-making.

## Key Points

- Economists argue for 'looking forward' from the current moment.
- Often mistakenly included in cost calculations (Sunk Cost Fallacy).
- Relevant to business continuation decisions.
- Mental accounting often makes it difficult to ignore sunk costs.
- Sunk cost bias can lead to doubling down on failing projects.

## Examples

- Movie production sets, R&D expenditures that cannot be licensed.
- Losing a ticket for a concert and deciding whether to buy a new one.
- Continuing a failing research project because of time already invested.

## Notes

Firms should decide based on future relevant costs rather than sunk past expenditures. There is a distinction between accounting for sunk costs and learning from past mistakes.
