## Definition

The foundational economic framework used to model market behavior where the supply curve represents the quantity producers are willing to provide at various prices, and the demand curve represents the quantity consumers are willing to purchase.

## Key Points

- Supply curves are typically upward sloping.
- Demand curves are typically downward sloping.
- Prices change based on shifts in either curve due to external shocks.
- Equilibrium occurs where the quantity demanded equals the quantity supplied.
- Shifts in supply or demand curves are driven by factors like technological innovation, changes in consumer preferences, or input costs.
- Prices act as signals to coordinate between producers and consumers in a market economy.

## Formulas & Equations

- S(P) = Quantity supplied
- D(P) = Quantity demanded
- Equilibrium: Qd = Qs

## Examples

- Rides to the airport market.
- Cheeseboard pizza market.
- International copper market price stability despite a 100-fold increase in consumption.
- San Francisco housing market dynamics where price stability with lower sales volume suggests simultaneous leftward shifts in both supply and demand.
