# Tax Incidence

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Fall 2024 - EWMBA 201A-LEC-31A _ 32A - Economics for Business Decision Making_Captions_English (United States) (1).txt

## Definition

The analysis of who effectively bears the economic burden of a tax, which is independent of which party formally remits the payment to the government.

## Key Points

- Tax burden depends on the relative price elasticities of supply and demand
- The more inelastic party bears a larger share of the tax burden
- Taxes create a wedge between the price paid by consumers and the price received by producers
- Taxes typically generate deadweight loss unless one side is perfectly inelastic

## Formulas & Equations

- PB - PS = T (where PB is price paid, PS is price received, and T is tax)

## Examples

- Sales tax
- Excise tax on gasoline
- Import tariffs
