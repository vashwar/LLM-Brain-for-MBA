# The Blue-Sky Paradox

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** TheyBoughtInNowTheyWantToBailOutHBRCaseStudy (1).pdf

## Definition

A management dilemma where stakeholders are encouraged to 'dream big' to secure initial project buy-in, only to face disappointment later when real-world constraints like budget, time, and technical limitations narrow the scope of what is actually achievable.

## Key Points

- Initial brainstorming often deliberately ignores restrictions to foster creativity.
- Budgetary, temporal, and legacy system constraints act as 'filters' that reduce the realm of possibility.
- The gap between the initial 'fantasy' version of a project and the 'real world' version leads to a loss of stakeholder faith.
- Stakeholders become emotionally and financially attached to specific 'wish list' items that may ultimately be cut.
