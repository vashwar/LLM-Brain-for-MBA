# The Illusion of Explanatory Depth

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** PersuadingTheUnpersuadable.pdf

## Definition

A cognitive bias where people believe they understand complex mechanisms in detail until they are forced to explain them step-by-step, at which point their overconfidence diminishes.

## Key Points

- Directly calling out ignorance triggers defensiveness; asking for an explanation triggers self-realization.
- The struggle to articulate a process humbles the individual and opens them to learning from others.
- This is a primary tool for managing 'know-it-alls'.
