# Treasury Stock

**Course:** Financial Accounting
**Source:** Session 07.pdf, Spring 2025 - EWMBA 202-LEC-31A _ 32A - Financial Accounting (Spring 2025)_Captions_English (United States) (4).txt

## Definition
A contra-equity account representing the cumulative amount a company has spent to repurchase its own shares from the marketplace to hold in its own treasury.

## Key Points
- Repurchased shares are effectively retired and lose voting and dividend rights.
- Reduces the size of stockholders' equity.
- Shown as a negative amount in the shareholders' equity section of the balance sheet.
- Often used to offset dilutive effects of share-based compensation or returning cash to shareholders.

## Formulas & Equations
- Outstanding Shares = Issued Shares - Treasury Shares

## Examples
- Nvidia repurchasing 342 million shares
- Apple repurchasing and retiring 1,000 shares
- A company buying back 3 million shares to offset executive stock options

## Notes
- Reissuance of treasury stock at a price different from cost affects Additional Paid-In-Capital (APIC), not income.
- The professor noted that many modern tech companies officially retire shares rather than keeping a Treasury stock account.
