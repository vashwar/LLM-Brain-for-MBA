# Two-Part Tariff

## Definition

A pricing strategy where a consumer pays a fixed initial fee for the right to purchase a good and a separate variable usage fee for each unit consumed.

## Key Points

- Can capture more profit than simple linear pricing.
- Allows the seller to capture consumer surplus.
- Fixed fee can be set to extract maximum surplus.
- Usage fee is often set at the marginal cost to prevent inefficient over-consumption.
- Often hidden in commercial products.
- Per-unit charge (set at marginal cost) incentivizes optimal consumption levels.
- Prevents wasteful overconsumption compared to flat-fee models.

## Formulas & Equations

- Price = Fixed Fee + (Usage Fee * Quantity)
- Profit = (Fixed Fee) + (Usage Fee * Quantity) - (Total Cost)

## Examples

- Starbucks lattes with different sizes
- Subscription services with usage fees
- Club memberships with per-visit fees
- Software licensing with per-user fees
- Moviepass
- Movie theater membership models
- Audible subscription model (fixed fee plus per-book cost)
