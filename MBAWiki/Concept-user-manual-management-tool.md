## Definition

A User Manual is a short, reflective document used by leaders to communicate their work style, values, preferences, and quirks to their team, helping members navigate the leader's personality more effectively.

## Key Points

- **Efficiency:** Shortens the learning curve for new teams and employees by making implicit preferences explicit.
- **Content:** Typically includes communication preferences (e.g., email vs. Slack), what the leader has no patience for, quirks that might be misunderstood, and how others can best assist them.
- **Transparency:** Promotes transparency regarding work habits and values.
- **Maintenance:** Ideally treated as a living document that adapts as roles, team dynamics, or contexts change.

## Examples

- A VP stating in their manual that they value speed over perfection and prefer quick, bulleted email responses over long meetings.
- The CEO of GitLab maintaining a public page detailing his management philosophy, decision-making style, and personal quirks to provide radical transparency to all employees.
