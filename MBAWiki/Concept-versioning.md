# Versioning

**Course:** Microeconomics
**Source:** Slides Week 3 - with notes.pdf, Slides Week 7 - with notes.pdf

## Definition

A strategy where a firm offers multiple versions of a product at different price points to segment consumers based on their willingness to pay and price elasticities.

## Key Points

- Also known as second-degree price discrimination.
- Used to capture consumer surplus.
- Targets different customer segments.
- Works best when marginal costs are low.
- Firms may intentionally degrade the 'basic' version to prevent premium customers from switching.
- Allows consumers to self-select into the version that best fits their budget and needs.
