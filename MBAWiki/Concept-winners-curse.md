## Definition

A phenomenon in common value auctions where the winner of an auction or competition often pays more than the item is actually worth, or experiences negative outcomes because they over-committed. The winner tends to be the one who most significantly overestimates the item's value.

## Key Points

- Occurs due to irrational bidding or poor information.
- Related to the difficulty in assessing true value.
- Occurs in common value auctions where the true value is uncertain.
- Rational bidders should account for this by shading their bids downward.
