# Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA)

**Course:** Leading People
**Source:** LP_2024_class_9_WE.pdf, Chapter10MakingRationalDecisionsInNegotiations.pdf

## Definition

The range of possible settlements where the reservation points of two negotiating parties overlap. It is the range between the parties' reservation prices where a mutually beneficial agreement is possible.

## Key Points

- Also known as the bargaining zone.
- An agreement is only possible if there is an overlap between the buyer's maximum price and the seller's minimum price.
- **Positive Bargaining Zone:** Exists when the buyer's maximum [[Reservation Price]] is higher than the seller's minimum. Any point within this overlap is a potential agreement that beats both parties' [[BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)]].
- **Negative Bargaining Zone:** Exists when reservation points do not overlap, making a rational agreement unlikely/impossible.

## Formulas & Equations

- ZOPA = [Seller's Reservation Price, Buyer's Reservation Price] (if Buyer RP > Seller RP)

## Examples

- An employer is willing to pay up to $97,000 (Employer's RP) and a recruit is willing to accept as little as $93,000 (Recruit's RP). The ZOPA is $93,000 - $97,000.

## Notes

The framework assumes negotiators prefer impasse over settling outside their reservation points. Referred to in some texts simply as the "Bargaining Zone."
