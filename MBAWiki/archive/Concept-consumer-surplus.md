# Consumer Surplus

**Source:** Slides Week 1 Weekend - with notes.pdf

## Definition
Consumer surplus is the difference between what a consumer is willing to pay for a good or service and what they actually pay. It represents the net benefit or happiness that consumers receive from purchasing a product.

## Key Points
- The happiness left over to the consumers after they pay for a product.
- Calculated as the difference between WTP and the actual price.
- Total consumer surplus is the sum of individual consumer surpluses.
- Represented by the area below the demand curve and above the price.

## Step-by-Step Example
If Ricardo values a slice of pizza at $12 and the price is $3, his consumer surplus is $9. The total consumer surplus for a group is the sum of each person's individual surplus.
