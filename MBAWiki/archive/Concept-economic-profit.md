# Economic Profit

**Source:** Slides Week 1 Weekend - with notes.pdf

## Definition
Economic profit is the difference between total revenue and the sum of explicit and implicit costs. It represents the true profitability of a business or investment, considering all opportunity costs.

## Key Points
- Total revenue minus explicit and implicit costs.
- Reflects true profitability.
- Useful for investment decisions.
- Often different from accounting profit.
- Economic profit is useful to decide whether to invest in a project or open a new store.

## Step-by-Step Example
Choosing entrepreneurship over a consulting job, where the net explicit benefit of entrepreneurship is $100,000 and the net explicit benefit of the consulting job is $150,000. Choosing entrepreneurship results in a net loss of $50,000, which is the economic profit.

## Real-World Applications
- Investment analysis
- Business valuation
- Strategic decision-making
