# Elasticity

**Source:** Slides Week 2 with notes.pdf

## Definition

Elasticity measures the responsiveness of one variable to changes in another variable. It is calculated as the percentage change in one variable divided by the percentage change in another variable.

## Key Points

- Elasticity is a unit-free measure.
- Income elasticity of demand measures the sensitivity of quantity demanded to changes in income.
- Own-price elasticity of demand measures the sensitivity of quantity demanded to changes in the price of the good.

## Formulas & Equations

- Elasticity = (% change in variable 1) / (% change in variable 2)

## Examples

- Tesla Model 3 as an example for income elasticity and luxury goods.
- Ramen noodles as an example of an inferior good.
- Own-price elasticity calculation for Model 3 based on price increase and demand decrease.
