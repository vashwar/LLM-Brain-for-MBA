# Marginal Cost

**Source:** Slides Week 1 Weekend - with notes.pdf

## Definition
Marginal cost is the change in total cost that results from producing one more unit of a good or service. It measures the incremental cost of increasing production.

## Key Points
- The incremental cost from an additional unit
- Change in total cost from producing one more unit.
- Used for optimal production decisions.
- Can differ from average cost.
- You need to stop at the point at which Marginal Benefits is equal to the Marginal Cost.

## Step-by-Step Example
The total cost of one session is $26 and the total cost of two sessions is $48. The marginal cost of the second session is $48 - $26 = $22.
